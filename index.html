Democratizing credit approvals

Allowing merchants to increase their sales by providing financing to their customers.

Easy

Proton Pay is designed to make offering financing incredibly easy for merchants. By using a single, streamlined credit application, merchants don’t have to spend time manually submitting applications to different lenders or tracking multiple responses. Proton Pay handles the process in one step, automatically connecting with multiple lenders to provide a curated list of financing options with various rates. This means merchants can offer their customers more choices quickly and efficiently, all while minimizing their administrative workload and enhancing the overall buying experience. It's a one-stop solution that maximizes convenience and potential conversion rates.

Fast

Proton Pay is fast for merchants because it consolidates the entire financing process into one seamless step. Instead of spending hours or even days coordinating with multiple lenders, merchants can simply submit a single credit application through Proton Pay. Our system immediately reaches out to a network of lenders and quickly returns a list of approvals with competitive rates. This accelerated process not only saves merchants valuable time but also keeps customers engaged by offering near-instant financing options, which can lead to faster decision-making and higher conversion rates.

FAQ

What is Proton Pay?
Proton Pay is a real-time matching engine that connects customers with the ideal lender by offering dynamic, waterfall-style lending. This approach maximizes approval rates by tapping into a broad spectrum of financial products, tailored specifically to meet each customer’s unique financing needs.
How does Proton Pay works?
At checkout, customers complete a secure application that connects them to a network of lenders, offering the best financing options based on their credit profile.
What type of customers can Proton Pay service?
Proton Pay can service a wide range of customers across the credit spectrum, from prime to subprime borrowers. This includes customers with strong credit, those with near-prime credit, and even those with limited or no credit history.
